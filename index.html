12-Week Curriculum

Week 1 FREE TRIAL

Welcome & Placement

Level placement test, course overview and goals, introduction to English sounds and alphabet, basic greetings and self-introduction

Mon: Pronunciation & sounds
Wed: Greetings & introductions
Fri: Mini presentation — "About Me"
Week 2

Present Tenses & Daily Life

Present Simple (habits, facts, routines), Present Continuous (actions happening now), key vocabulary for daily routines and time expressions, listening to short dialogues

Mon: Present Simple
Wed: Present Continuous
Fri: Speaking practice — describe your day
Week 3

Past Tenses & Storytelling

Past Simple (regular & irregular verbs), Past Continuous (interrupted actions), vocabulary with time markers (yesterday, last week, ago), writing a short paragraph about a past event

Mon: Past Simple
Wed: Past Continuous
Fri: Story writing workshop
Week 4

Future Tenses & Plans

Will (predictions & decisions), Going to (plans & intentions), Present Continuous for future arrangements, speaking about goals and dreams

Mon: Will vs Going To
Wed: Arrangements & plans
Fri: Debate — "What will the future look like?"
Week 5

Modal Verbs & Expressing Ideas

Can/Could (ability & possibility), Must/Have to (obligation), Should/Ought to (advice), May/Might (probability), expressing opinions and giving advice

Mon: Can/Could/Must
Wed: Should/May/Might
Fri: Role-play — giving advice
Week 6

Vocabulary Expansion

Word families (noun/verb/adjective/adverb), prefixes and suffixes, synonyms and antonyms, collocations and fixed phrases, top 30 most common phrasal verbs

Mon: Word building
Wed: Collocations & phrasal verbs
Fri: Vocabulary quiz game
Week 7

Reading Comprehension

Skimming (reading for gist), scanning (reading for specific info), identifying main idea and supporting details, understanding unknown words from context, True/False, MCQ, and open-ended question types

Mon: Skimming & scanning
Wed: Inference & context clues
Fri: Full comprehension practice
Week 8

Writing Skills Part 1

Sentence structure (simple, compound, complex), paragraph structure (topic sentence, body, conclusion), linking words and transition phrases, punctuation rules, writing a descriptive paragraph

Mon: Sentence & paragraph structure
Wed: Linking words & punctuation
Fri: Write & peer-review a paragraph
Week 9

Advanced Grammar

Conditionals (Zero, First, Second, Third), passive voice (all tenses), relative clauses (defining & non-defining), reported speech

Mon: Conditionals
Wed: Passive & relative clauses
Fri: Grammar challenge
Week 10

Writing Skills Part 2

Essay types (argumentative, narrative, descriptive, opinion), essay structure (intro, body, conclusion), thesis statements and topic sentences, counterarguments, timed writing practice (30-minute essays)

Mon: Essay structure & types
Wed: Thesis & argumentation
Fri: Timed essay + instructor feedback
Week 11

Speaking & Listening Mastery

Presentation skills (structure, delivery, body language), active listening strategies, discussion and debate techniques, pronunciation refinement, TED talk analysis

Mon: Presentation skills
Wed: Debate techniques
Fri: Student presentations
Week 12

Final Review & Certification

Comprehensive grammar revision, full mock exam (reading + writing + speaking), vocabulary final quiz, individual feedback session, certificate of completion awarded

Mon: Full review
Wed: Mock exam
Fri: Certificates & celebration session

12-Week BAC Curriculum

Week 1 FREE TRIAL

Introduction & Diagnosis

BAC exam format explained in full detail, what examiners look for (marking criteria), level diagnostic test, Present Simple and Continuous review

Sun: BAC format overview
Tue: Diagnostic test
Sat: Tenses review
Week 2

All Tenses Mastery

Present Simple, Continuous, Perfect; Past Simple, Continuous, Perfect; Future (Will, Going to, Present Continuous); BAC-style grammar exercises; common tense mistakes Algerian students make

Sun: Present tenses
Tue: Past tenses
Sat: Future tenses + mixed practice
Week 3

Modal Verbs & Passive Voice

Modal verbs (can, could, must, should, may, might, would), passive voice formation and use in all tenses, how modals and passive appear in BAC reading texts

Sun: Modal verbs
Tue: Passive voice
Sat: BAC exercises on modals & passive
Week 4

Conditionals & Reported Speech

Zero, First, Second, Third conditionals, mixed conditionals, reported speech (statements, questions, commands), common BAC topics that use conditionals (science, environment, technology)

Sun: Conditionals 0 & 1
Tue: Conditionals 2 & 3
Sat: Reported speech + BAC practice
Week 5

Vocabulary for BAC Topics

Word families, prefixes and suffixes, synonyms/antonyms for high-frequency BAC words, key topic vocabulary (Technology, Environment, Health, Education, Globalization, Science), collocations in BAC texts

Sun: Word building
Tue: Topic vocab — Tech & Environment
Sat: Topic vocab — Health & Education
Week 6

Reading Comprehension Techniques

How to approach Part 1 (time management: 1h30 for 14pts), skimming for general idea, scanning for specific info, True/False/Not Given strategy, multiple choice strategy

Sun: Skimming & scanning
Tue: T/F and MCQ strategies
Sat: Full reading comprehension practice
Week 7

Advanced Reading & Vocabulary Questions

Inference questions (reading between the lines), vocabulary-in-context questions (find synonym/meaning), reference questions (what does "it/they/this" refer to?), titling and summarising, BAC past papers 2022 & 2023

Sun: Inference & reference
Tue: Vocabulary questions
Sat: Full past paper (timed)
Week 8

Writing Foundations (Part 2 Intro)

Introduction to BAC Part 2 Written Expression (1h30, 6pts), how to choose from 3–4 essay topics, essay types (argumentative, opinion, narrative, letter), essay structure (intro, 2–3 body paragraphs, conclusion), linking words

Sun: Essay structure
Tue: Linking words & essay types
Sat: Write intro + conclusion (feedback)
Week 9

Argumentative & Opinion Essays

How to write a strong BAC argumentative essay, thesis statement writing, presenting arguments with evidence, counterarguments and refutation, opinion essay ("I think/believe/feel"), common BAC topics (social media, technology, environment, youth, gender equality)

Sun: Argumentative essay structure
Tue: Practice essay (technology)
Sat: Peer feedback + instructor correction
Week 10

Narrative & Letter Writing

Narrative essay (structure, past tenses, sequencing), descriptive writing (adjectives, sensory language), formal letter writing (format, register, closing phrases), informal letter (tone, expressions, layout)

Sun: Narrative essay
Tue: Descriptive writing
Sat: Letter writing (formal + informal)
Week 11

Full Exam Simulations

Full BAC mock exam 1 (Part 1 + Part 2 under real conditions), full BAC mock exam 2 (different paper, timed), detailed analysis of where marks are lost and gained, individual written feedback on essays

Sun: Mock exam 1
Tue: Correction & analysis
Sat: Mock exam 2
Week 12

Final Revision & Confidence Building

Comprehensive grammar quick-reference revision, top 50 vocabulary words for BAC, reading comprehension final drills, essay writing final tips and common mistakes, exam day advice (mindset, time, what to bring), certificate awarded

Sun: Grammar & vocab revision
Tue: Full reading drill
Sat: Final session + certificates + motivation
